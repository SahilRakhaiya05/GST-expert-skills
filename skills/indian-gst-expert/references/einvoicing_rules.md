## 1. What Is E-Invoicing?

E-invoicing under GST is a system where B2B (business-to-business) invoices are:
1. Generated by the supplier in a **standardized JSON format** (Form GST INV-01)
2. **Uploaded to the Invoice Registration Portal (IRP)**
3. Authenticated by the IRP, which assigns a unique **Invoice Reference Number (IRN)**
4. Returned to the supplier with a **digitally signed QR code** and IRN embedded

Without a valid IRN, an invoice is **legally invalid** for GST purposes — the buyer **cannot claim Input Tax Credit (ITC)** on it.

## 2. Applicability & Thresholds

### Current Mandatory Threshold (2026)

| Threshold | Requirement | Effective Since |
|---|---|---|
| AATO > ₹5 Crore | **Mandatory e-invoicing** for all B2B invoices | August 1, 2023 |
| AATO > ₹10 Crore | **30-day IRP reporting window** (in addition to e-invoicing) | April 1, 2025 |

**AATO** = Annual Aggregate Turnover = Total turnover across all GSTINs under a single PAN, in any financial year since FY 2017-18.

> Businesses that crossed ₹5 Crore in **any** financial year since 2017-18 must comply — even if current year turnover is lower.

### Historical Threshold Timeline

| Period | Threshold |
|---|---|
| Oct 2020 | ₹500 Crore |
| Jan 2021 | ₹100 Crore |
| Apr 2021 | ₹50 Crore |
| Oct 2021 | ₹20 Crore |
| Apr 2022 | ₹10 Crore |
| Aug 2023 | ₹5 Crore (current) |

### Transaction Types Covered

E-invoicing applies to:
- **B2B invoices** (supply to registered GST persons)
- **B2G invoices** (supply to government departments)
- **Exports** (with or without payment of tax)
- **SEZ supplies** (with or without payment of tax)
- **Credit Notes and Debit Notes** linked to eligible invoices
- **Reverse Charge** invoices issued by registered recipients

E-invoicing does **NOT** apply to:
- B2C invoices (supply to unregistered consumers)
- Nil-rated/exempt supplies only

## 3. 30-Day IRP Reporting Rule

### Who It Applies To
Businesses with AATO ≥ ₹10 Crore (effective April 1, 2025).

### What It Means
- Any invoice, credit note, or debit note dated more than **30 days ago** from today **will be rejected by the IRP**.
- The IRP will **not generate an IRN** for late-uploaded documents.
- Late invoices are **invalid for GST and ITC purposes**.

### Practical Impact
| Scenario | Result |
|---|---|
| Invoice dated May 1, uploaded May 25 | ✅ Accepted (within 30 days) |
| Invoice dated May 1, uploaded June 2 | ❌ Rejected by IRP (32 days) |
| Invoice dated March 15, uploaded April 16 | ❌ Rejected (32 days) |

### How to Fix a Rejected Invoice
1. Cancel the original (rejected) invoice
2. Issue a **fresh invoice with a new invoice number and today's date**
3. Upload the new invoice to IRP within 30 days of the new date
4. Communicate the new invoice details to the buyer

> **Warning:** Backdating invoices to fit within the 30-day window is illegal and can attract scrutiny and penalties.

## 4. How E-Invoicing Works (Step-by-Step)

```
Supplier creates invoice in accounting software
        ↓
Invoice data formatted as JSON (Form GST INV-01)
        ↓
JSON uploaded to IRP (via API, GST Suvidha Provider, or direct portal)
        ↓
IRP validates data (GSTIN, tax amounts, HSN codes, etc.)
        ↓
IRP generates:  IRN (unique 64-character hash)
                Signed QR Code (with key invoice details)
                Digitally signed e-invoice JSON
        ↓
IRP auto-populates:  GSTR-1 (Part A)
                     E-Way Bill (if applicable — consignment value > ₹50,000)
        ↓
Supplier shares invoice (PDF/printed) with embedded IRN + QR Code to buyer
```

### IRN Generation Formula
IRN = SHA-256 hash of: Supplier GSTIN + Financial Year + Document Type + Document Number

## 5. Mandatory Fields on E-Invoice

### Supplier Details
- Supplier GSTIN
- Supplier legal name
- Supplier address (complete)
- Supplier state code

### Buyer Details
- Buyer GSTIN (for B2B)
- Buyer legal name
- Buyer billing address
- Buyer state code
- Place of supply

### Invoice Details
- Invoice number (unique per GSTIN per FY)
- Invoice date
- Invoice type (Regular / Export / SEZ / Deemed Export / Credit Note / Debit Note)
- HSN/SAC code (4 or 6 digits per turnover threshold)
- Item description
- Quantity and unit
- Unit price
- Taxable value
- Discount (if any)
- CGST/SGST or IGST rate and amount
- Cess (if applicable)
- Total invoice value
- Reverse charge indicator (Yes/No)
- E-way bill details (if applicable)

## 6. Exempted Categories

The following are **exempt from e-invoicing** even if AATO > ₹5 Crore:

| Category | Reason |
|---|---|
| Goods Transport Agencies (GTA) | Covered under different system |
| Passenger transport services | Consumer-facing |
| Banks and NBFCs | Financial sector exemption |
| Insurance companies | Financial sector exemption |
| Multiplexes / cinema halls | Specific notification |
| SEZ units (as suppliers) | Special economic zone exemption |
| Government departments | Exempted by notification |
| B2C invoices (any supplier) | Not applicable to consumers |
| Composition scheme dealers | Exempt (below threshold effectively) |

## 7. Penalties for Non-Compliance

| Violation | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Invoice without valid IRN | Up to ₹10,000 per invoice OR 100% of tax (whichever higher) |
| Incorrect invoice details | Up to ₹25,000 per incorrect invoice |
| Buyer's ITC denied | Full ITC amount + interest @18% p.a. |
| Late IRP upload (>30 days for ₹10 Cr+ AATO) | Invoice rejected (no IRN) → supply legally invalid |
| Detention of goods | Applicable if e-way bill auto-generation fails due to invalid e-invoice |

> **Section 122 of CGST Act** governs penalties for e-invoice non-compliance.

## 8. E-Way Bill Integration

When an e-invoice is generated for consignments with value > ₹50,000:
- The **e-way bill is auto-generated** using the data already provided in the e-invoice
- No separate e-way bill generation is needed
- The IRN from the e-invoice becomes the basis for the e-way bill

> If e-way bill details (transporter ID, vehicle number) are provided at the time of IRN generation, the system generates both simultaneously.

## 9. Invoice Registration Portals (IRPs)

As of 2026, the government has authorized multiple IRPs:

| IRP | URL |
|---|---|
| NIC IRP 1 (primary) | einvoice1.gst.gov.in |
| NIC IRP 2 | einvoice2.gst.gov.in |
| IRIS IRP | einvoiceirp.iris.in |
| ClearTax IRP | einvoice.cleartax.in |
| Cygnet IRP | einvoice.cygnet.in |
| EY IRP | einvoice.eygbs.in |

Any authorized IRP can be used — the IRN generated is identical and valid across all portals.

## 10. Common Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Wrong GSTIN of buyer | IRP rejection | Verify buyer GSTIN on gst.gov.in before invoicing |
| HSN code mismatch | IRP rejection / ITC risk | Use 4 or 6 digit HSN as per AATO threshold |
| Uploading invoice >30 days old (₹10 Cr+ AATO) | IRP rejection | Issue fresh invoice with new date |
| Not embedding IRN/QR on printed invoice | Penalty risk | Print PDF from IRP or use GST software with auto-embed |
| Generating IRN but not sharing with buyer | Buyer ITC risk | Always share IRN + QR with buyer immediately |
| Duplicate invoice number | IRP rejection | Maintain sequential invoice numbering |
| Incorrect tax amounts | Validation failure | Use GST software or double-check CGST/SGST/IGST split |
| Missing place of supply | IRP rejection | Always include state code of supply destination |
